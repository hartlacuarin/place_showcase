The Heart of Switzerland Awaits

Discover Lucerne, a stunning city where the past and present harmonize perfectly. Walk across its iconic wooden bridges, gaze upon the turquoise waters of the lake, and find yourself captivated by the breathtaking backdrop of snow-capped Alpine peaks. Your Swiss adventure begins here.

Lucerne's Key Attractions

Chapel Bridge (Kapellbrücke)

As Europe’s oldest wooden covered bridge, the Kapellbrücke is the centerpiece of Lucerne. Originally built in the 14th century, it features a series of interior pictorial panels dating back to the 17th century, illustrating Lucerne's history and mythology.

Though partially destroyed by fire in 1993, it was meticulously restored and stands today as a powerful symbol of the city's resilience. The adjacent octagonal Water Tower once served as a prison, treasury, and watchtower.

Lion Monument (Löwendenkmal)

Carved directly into a sandstone cliff, the Lion Monument is one of the world's most famous memorials. It commemorates the Swiss Guards massacred in 1792 during the French Revolution when defending the royal family.

Mark Twain described the sculpture of the dying lion as the "saddest and most moving piece of rock in the world." It is a powerful and somber tribute to courage and loyalty.

Mount Pilatus

Dominating the Lucerne skyline, Mount Pilatus offers a truly golden round trip experience. Visitors can ascend via the world's steepest cogwheel railway and descend by aerial gondolas or panorama cable cars.

Legend says the mountain is named after Pontius Pilate and was once home to dragons with healing powers. At its peak, you'll find panoramic views of 73 Alpine peaks and the surrounding lakes.

Lake Lucerne (Vierwaldstättersee)

This beautiful, irregularly shaped lake, the fourth largest in Switzerland, is ringed by dramatic mountains and offers stunning, fjord-like scenery. Its name translates to "Lake of the Four Forest Cantons."

The most popular way to experience the lake is by taking a historic paddle steamer or modern motor vessel cruise, connecting Lucerne with scenic towns and the cable car bases for the surrounding peaks.

Old Town & Musegg Wall

Lucerne's Altstadt (Old Town) is a pedestrian paradise, known for its colorful, historic buildings adorned with elaborate frescoes and situated along the Reuss River. It's the perfect place for a leisurely stroll.

Overlooking the Old Town is the Musegg Wall, a well-preserved section of the medieval fortifications. Several of its nine towers are open to the public, offering fantastic panoramic views of the city, lake, and mountains.
